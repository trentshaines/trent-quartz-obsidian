[[Lewis Carroll]]

In the dialogue, the Tortoise challenges Achilles about accepting logical inferences. The Tortoise accepts the premises of an argument but refuses to accept that the conclusion follows from them without adding an additional premise that explicitly states that the conclusion follows from the premises. This creates an infinite regress, because each new premise would itself require another premise to justify the inference.
